\begin{abstract}
We propose a locality-biased sparse Transformer for long-document classification. We benchmark against five strong baselines (Longformer, BigBird, Linformer, Performer, vanilla Transformer) on three datasets. Our method achieves new SOTA on HyperNews-10K while reducing memory by 38\%.
\end{abstract}

\section{Method}
We replace full self-attention with a locality-biased kernel:
\begin{equation}
\text{Attention}_{\text{ours}}(Q, K, V) = \text{softmax}\!\left(\frac{Q K^\top}{\sqrt{d_k}} + \lambda B\right) V
\end{equation}
where $B_{ij} = -|i-j|/s$ is a learned locality prior with scale parameter $s$, and $\lambda$ balances the standard attention against the locality bias.

Our full model achieves 81.4 F1 on HyperNews-10K, outperforming all baselines. Ablations show that removing the locality bias ($\lambda=0$) drops F1 by 5.5 points, and removing sparsity drops it by 2.2 points.

\section{Discussion}
Our method achieves SOTA with 38\% less memory than BigBird.
